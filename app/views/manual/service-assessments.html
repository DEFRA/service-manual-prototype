Standards and assurance

Service assessments

All transactional government digital services need an independent assessment to ensure they are fit for purpose, technically robust, and secure.

When you need an assessment

You will need a service assessment if your service:

  • is going on GOV.UK
  • has a spend approval condition that requires assessment
  • is transactional (it involves money, data, or a change of circumstances)

How to book

{{ govukInsetText({ text: "Assessments are arranged by the DTSA (Digital Technical Service Assessment) team." }) }}

The type of panel you get depends on your transaction volume:

  • over 100k transactions a year: assessed by a cross-government (GDS/CDDO) panel
  • under 100k transactions a year: assessed internally by a Defra panel

Lead times

Internal assessors are volunteers. You must give a minimum of 6 weeks notice. We will try to book an appointment within a 2-week window of your request date.

Email to book

Send your request to serviceassessments@defra.gov.uk with:

  • name of the service
  • type of assessment required (Alpha or Beta)
  • a short summary of what the service does
  • a 2-week period when you are ready to be assessed

Exemptions

All central government services must be on GOV.UK unless they meet specific exemption criteria (for example, it is for internal staff only).

To request an exemption:

  1. Check the GOV.UK proposition to see if you qualify.
  2. Email serviceassessments@defra.gov.uk with the subject "Exemption request: [Service Name]".
  3. Include your proposed domain name and evidence of why user needs cannot be met on GOV.UK.
! Warning Do not procure an external website until your exemption request has been approved.
