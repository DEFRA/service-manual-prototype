Sustainability

Defra’s services aim to bring tangible environmental benefits to our society. Your digital service must also minimise its own environmental and social costs.

The use of technology contributes to climate change through energy use, resource depletion, and biodiversity loss. Service teams must design, develop, and operate services to be as sustainable as possible.

Strategic objectives (2025 to 2030)

Defra’s Digital Sustainability Strategy lays out 6 strategic objectives for the sustainable use of technology.

How to meet the standard

There are 3 broad steps that services need to go through for the end-to-end sustainability process in Defra.

1. Digital sustainability risk evaluation

When: Before the start of Alpha

You should complete a risk evaluation to identify the main sustainability risks your project will likely face. This process suggests actions to reduce or avoid these risks.

2. Digital sustainability statement

When: From Alpha onwards

Each service should fill out and update a Digital Sustainability Statement. This documents the actions you have taken and those planned for future phases against the 6 strategic objectives.

If there are no actions appropriate for your service in a specific objective, you should record this in the statement.

3. Service assessment (The 15th Point)

From 1 April 2026, a new 15th Service Standard point focused on sustainability will be mandated for Defra service assessments.

Initially, you will receive an 'advisory' RAG rating (Red/Amber/Green) for this point. It will not impact your overall assessment result but will reflect the assessors' confidence in your sustainability actions.

Metrics and measurement

You should record what you plan to measure in your Sustainability Statement. However, we do not currently expect services to have a developed carbon metric.

Many meaningful actions can be taken without measurement, such as:

  • reducing data capture and storage
  • turning off environments when not in use
  • re-using components rather than developing from scratch
  • ensuring hardware is treated in a circular manner
