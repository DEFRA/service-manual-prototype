Service Standard

15. Deliver a sustainable service

Understand any social and environmental risks created by your service and take steps to reduce these as far as possible.

Defra adheres to the cross-government GOV.UK Service Standard (opens in new tab). All services must meet the standard's 14 points.

Because of Defra's role in protecting the environment, services must also meet this 15th point about sustainability.

Why it's important

Defra's services aim to bring tangible environmental benefits to our society â€“ from improving water quality to boosting the nation's resilience to flooding. Benefits can also come from replacing existing processes that are relatively resource intensive with more lightweight and modern equivalents.

However, the use of technology for government services also contributes to climate change and other sustainability risks.

  • Energy use contributes to warming greenhouse gases.
  • Hardware manufacturing (mining and processing) can lead to water scarcity, resource depletion, and biodiversity loss.
  • Supply chains can have unintended social impacts, such as labour exploitation.

What it means

Service teams should design, develop, and operate their services to be as sustainable as possible, and set out their approach in a sustainability statement.

The statement should lay out what actions have been taken, and those that are planned, against the 6 objectives of Defra's Digital Sustainability Strategy (opens in new tab).

Detailed guidance

More detailed guidance on reducing the impacts of services through their lifecycle can be found in the Greener Service Principles (opens in new tab).

Guidance on artificial intelligence can be found in Defra's AI sustainability guidance (opens in new tab).
